# OPERATING AGREEMENT OF DEAL PULSE LLC

**A Kansas Limited Liability Company**

This Operating Agreement (this "Agreement") of **Deal Pulse LLC**, a Kansas limited liability company (the "Company"), is entered into and effective as of the date of the last signature below (the "Effective Date"), by and among the undersigned persons, each of whom is admitted as a Member of the Company (each a "Member," and collectively, the "Members").

> **NOTICE:** This template is provided for convenience only. It is not legal advice. The Members should have this Agreement reviewed by a Kansas-licensed attorney prior to execution.

---

## ARTICLE 1 — FORMATION

**1.1 Formation.** The Company was formed as a limited liability company under the Kansas Revised Limited Liability Company Act, K.S.A. §§ 17-7662 *et seq.* (the "Act"), by the filing of Articles of Organization with the Kansas Secretary of State.

**1.2 Name.** The name of the Company is **Deal Pulse LLC**.

**1.3 Principal Office.** The principal office of the Company shall be located in the State of Kansas at an address designated by the Members, or at such other place as the Members may from time to time determine.

**1.4 Registered Agent.** The Company's registered agent and registered office in Kansas shall be as stated in the Articles of Organization, as amended from time to time.

**1.5 Purpose.** The Company is formed for the purpose of engaging in any lawful act or activity for which a limited liability company may be organized under the Act, including without limitation the development, marketing, and licensing of software-as-a-service products for real estate professionals.

**1.6 Term.** The Company commenced on the date its Articles of Organization were filed with the Kansas Secretary of State and shall continue perpetually until dissolved in accordance with this Agreement or the Act.

**1.7 EIN.** The Company has obtained an Employer Identification Number (EIN) from the Internal Revenue Service.

## ARTICLE 3 — CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS

**3.1 Initial Capital Contributions.** Each Member has made, or shall make, the initial capital contribution (cash, property, or services) set forth on **Schedule A** attached hereto, in exchange for the Percentage Interest stated in Section 2.1.

**3.2 Additional Capital Contributions.** No Member shall be required to make any additional capital contribution. Additional contributions may be made only upon the unanimous written consent of the Members, and shall be made in proportion to each Member's Percentage Interest unless otherwise agreed in writing.

**3.3 Capital Accounts.** A separate capital account shall be maintained for each Member in accordance with U.S. Treasury Regulations § 1.704-1(b)(2)(iv), as amended.

**3.4 No Interest on Capital.** No Member shall be entitled to interest on that Member's capital contributions or capital account.

**3.5 No Withdrawal of Capital.** Except as expressly provided in this Agreement, no Member shall have the right to withdraw or demand the return of any capital contribution.

---

## ARTICLE 4 — ALLOCATIONS AND DISTRIBUTIONS

**4.1 Allocation of Profits and Losses.** Profits and losses of the Company for each fiscal year shall be allocated among the Members in proportion to their respective Percentage Interests.

**4.2 Distributions.** Subject to the Act and the retention of reasonable working capital reserves determined by the Members, the Company shall distribute available cash to the Members in proportion to their respective Percentage Interests, at such times as the Members may unanimously determine.

**4.3 Tax Distributions.** To the extent of available cash, the Company shall use reasonable efforts to distribute to each Member, prior to the due date of that Member's applicable quarterly estimated tax payment, an amount sufficient to cover the Member's estimated federal and state income tax liability arising from the Member's allocable share of Company taxable income, assuming the highest marginal individual tax rate applicable.

**4.4 Tax Treatment.** The Members intend that the Company be treated as a partnership for federal and state income tax purposes, and no Member shall take any action inconsistent with that treatment without the unanimous written consent of the Members.

---

## ARTICLE 5 — MANAGEMENT

**5.1 Member-Managed.** The Company shall be managed by its Members. There shall be no managers.

**5.2 Authority of Members.** Except as otherwise provided in this Agreement, the Members shall have full and complete authority, power, and discretion to manage and control the business, affairs, and properties of the Company, to make all decisions regarding those matters, and to perform any and all other acts or activities customary or incident to the management of the Company's business.

**5.3 Voting.** Each Member shall have voting rights in proportion to that Member's Percentage Interest. Except where this Agreement or the Act requires a greater vote, all actions requiring a vote of the Members shall require the affirmative vote of Members holding a majority of the Percentage Interests. **Because the Members each hold fifty percent (50%), any matter requiring a Member vote requires unanimous approval unless otherwise expressly provided herein.**

**5.4 Major Decisions.** Notwithstanding anything in this Agreement to the contrary, the following actions shall require the unanimous written consent of all Members:

(a) Admission of a new Member;
(b) Amendment of this Agreement or the Articles of Organization;
(c) Sale, lease, or other transfer of all or substantially all of the Company's assets;
(d) Incurrence of indebtedness in excess of $10,000 in any single transaction;
(e) Entering into any contract outside the ordinary course of business;
(f) Merger, consolidation, or conversion of the Company;
(g) Dissolution of the Company;
(h) Distribution of Company property;
(i) Election of tax classification.

**5.5 Deadlock.** In the event the Members are unable to reach agreement on a matter requiring unanimous consent for a period of sixty (60) consecutive days, either Member may initiate non-binding mediation in Sedgwick County, Kansas. If mediation does not resolve the deadlock within thirty (30) days thereafter, either Member may initiate the buy-sell procedure described on **Schedule B**.

**5.6 Officers.** The Members may, by unanimous consent, appoint one or more officers with such titles and authority as they shall determine.

**5.7 Devotion of Time.** Each Member shall devote such time to the Company as is reasonably necessary to carry out that Member's duties. No Member shall be required to devote full time to Company affairs, and each Member may engage in other business activities, whether or not competitive with the Company, provided such activities do not violate any separate written agreement between the Members.

**5.8 Compensation.** Members shall not receive compensation for services as Members, except as unanimously agreed in writing. Members shall be reimbursed for reasonable out-of-pocket expenses incurred on behalf of the Company.

---

## ARTICLE 6 — BOOKS, RECORDS, AND REPORTS

**6.1 Books of Account.** The Company shall maintain complete and accurate books and records at the Company's principal office. Each Member shall have the right to inspect and copy such books and records during normal business hours.

**6.2 Fiscal Year.** The fiscal year of the Company shall be the calendar year, unless otherwise required by the Internal Revenue Code.

**6.3 Tax Returns.** The Company shall cause to be prepared and timely filed all federal, state, and local tax returns required of the Company, and shall furnish each Member with a Schedule K-1 (or successor form) within a reasonable time after the end of each fiscal year.

**6.4 Partnership Representative.** The Members shall designate a "partnership representative" as required under Section 6223 of the Internal Revenue Code.

**6.5 Bank Accounts.** All Company funds shall be deposited in one or more accounts in the name of the Company at financial institutions selected by the Members.

---

## ARTICLE 7 — TRANSFERS OF MEMBERSHIP INTERESTS

**7.1 Restriction on Transfer.** No Member may sell, assign, pledge, encumber, or otherwise transfer all or any portion of that Member's Membership Interest without the prior written consent of the other Member, which consent may be withheld in the other Member's sole discretion.

**7.2 Right of First Refusal.** If a Member ("Selling Member") receives a bona fide written offer from a third party to purchase all or any portion of the Selling Member's Membership Interest, the Selling Member shall first offer the Interest in writing to the other Member on the same terms and conditions. The other Member shall have thirty (30) days to elect to purchase the offered Interest.

**7.3 Permitted Transfers.** Notwithstanding Section 7.1, a Member may transfer that Member's Membership Interest to a revocable living trust of which the Member is the sole grantor and primary beneficiary, provided the trustee agrees in writing to be bound by this Agreement.

**7.4 Prohibited Transferees.** No transfer shall be permitted to any person whose admission would cause the Company to be treated as a publicly traded partnership, terminate for federal tax purposes, or violate any applicable law.

---

## ARTICLE 8 — DISSOCIATION AND BUYOUT

**8.1 Events of Dissociation.** A Member shall be dissociated from the Company upon: (a) voluntary withdrawal with 90 days' written notice; (b) death or legal incapacity; (c) bankruptcy of the Member; or (d) unanimous vote of the other Members for cause, including material breach of this Agreement or willful misconduct harmful to the Company.

**8.2 Buyout of Dissociated Member.** Upon dissociation, the Company (or the remaining Members, pro rata) shall have the option, exercisable within ninety (90) days, to purchase the dissociated Member's Interest at fair market value determined by a mutually-selected qualified appraiser. Payment may be made over a period of up to thirty-six (36) months at the prime rate plus two percent (2%), with equal monthly installments.

**8.3 Effect of Dissociation.** A dissociated Member shall cease to have any rights as a Member, except the right to receive the buyout payments described above and any distributions declared before the date of dissociation.

---

## ARTICLE 9 — DISSOLUTION AND WINDING UP

**9.1 Events of Dissolution.** The Company shall dissolve upon: (a) the unanimous written consent of the Members; (b) entry of a decree of judicial dissolution under the Act; or (c) any other event causing dissolution under the Act that is not avoided by the continuation of the business.

**9.2 Winding Up.** Upon dissolution, the Company shall cease to carry on its business except as necessary to wind up, and shall proceed to liquidate its assets, pay or provide for its liabilities, and distribute the remaining assets to the Members in accordance with Section 9.3.

**9.3 Distribution of Assets.** After payment of, or adequate provision for, all debts and liabilities of the Company (including to Members), remaining assets shall be distributed to the Members in proportion to their positive capital account balances, determined after giving effect to all allocations for the year of liquidation.

**9.4 Articles of Dissolution.** Upon completion of winding up, the Members shall cause Articles of Dissolution to be filed with the Kansas Secretary of State.

---

## ARTICLE 10 — INDEMNIFICATION

**10.1 Indemnification.** To the fullest extent permitted by the Act, the Company shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless each Member from and against any loss, liability, damage, or expense (including reasonable attorneys' fees) incurred by the Member by reason of any act or omission performed in good faith on behalf of the Company and in a manner reasonably believed to be within the scope of authority conferred by this Agreement, except for acts of gross negligence, willful misconduct, or fraud.

**10.2 Limitation of Liability.** No Member shall be personally liable to the Company or the other Members for monetary damages for breach of fiduciary duty, except for acts or omissions constituting gross negligence, willful misconduct, fraud, or a knowing violation of law.

---

## ARTICLE 11 — CONFIDENTIALITY AND NON-SOLICITATION

**11.1 Confidentiality.** Each Member agrees to hold in strict confidence all non-public information about the Company, including customer lists, pricing, source code, MLS API credentials of the Company's customers, agent onboarding submissions, and business plans, and shall not use such information other than for the benefit of the Company.

**11.2 Non-Solicitation.** During the period in which a Member holds a Membership Interest and for one (1) year thereafter, no Member shall directly or indirectly solicit any customer or employee of the Company to terminate their relationship with the Company.

---

## ARTICLE 12 — GENERAL PROVISIONS

**12.1 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Kansas, without regard to conflict-of-laws principles.

**12.2 Venue.** Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be brought exclusively in the state or federal courts located in Sedgwick County, Kansas.

**12.3 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement (together with the Articles of Organization and any schedules hereto) constitutes the entire agreement among the Members with respect to the subject matter hereof, and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings, oral or written.

**12.4 Amendment.** This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by all Members.

**12.5 Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

**12.6 Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Electronic signatures shall be treated as originals.

**12.7 Notices.** All notices hereunder shall be in writing and delivered by hand, overnight courier, or email to the address listed in Section 2.1, and shall be deemed given upon receipt.

**12.8 Binding Effect.** This Agreement shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit of the Members and their respective heirs, legal representatives, successors, and permitted assigns.

## SCHEDULE B — BUY-SELL PROCEDURE (DEADLOCK)

In the event a deadlock is not resolved through mediation under Section 5.5, either Member (the "Offeror") may deliver a written offer (the "Offer") to the other Member (the "Offeree") stating a price per one percent (1%) of Percentage Interest (the "Stated Price"). Within thirty (30) days of receipt, the Offeree must elect, by written notice, either to:

(a) **Sell** the Offeree's entire Membership Interest to the Offeror at the Stated Price; or

(b) **Buy** the Offeror's entire Membership Interest at the Stated Price.

Closing of the elected transaction shall occur within sixty (60) days of the election notice. Payment shall be made in cash at closing, or on such other terms as the Members may agree in writing. If the Offeree fails to make an election within the thirty (30) day period, the Offeree shall be deemed to have elected to sell under clause (a).
